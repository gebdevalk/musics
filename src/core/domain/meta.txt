The parameters that are **inherently musical** are the ones that come from *music theory, notation, or performance practice* rather than from any technical protocol like MIDI. These describe how musicians think about sound, not how machines encode it.

---

## 🎼 Core musical‑notation parameters
These originate in traditional music theory and performance, independent of MIDI or any digital system.

- **dynamic** — expressive loudness markings (*p, mf, f, crescendo*).
- **articulation** — how a note is played (staccato, legato, marcato).
- **accent** — emphasis on a note (strong, weak, sforzando).
- **ornaments** — trills, mordents, turns, grace notes.
- **tie** — connecting two notes into one sustained sound.
- **slur** — phrasing across notes, indicating legato.
- **duration** — as a musical value (quarter note, eighth note), not as a MIDI timestamp.
- **pitch** — as a musical concept (C#, G♭), not the MIDI number.

(- **tuning** — musical pitch accuracy or temperament (just intonation, microtonal adjustments)).
(- **octave** — the musical register (C4, A5)).

These parameters exist in **sheet music**, **performance practice**, and **acoustic interpretation**, regardless of any digital encoding.

---

## 🎵 Performance‑practice parameters
These describe how a musician shapes a note physically or expressively.

- **attack** — how the note begins (soft, sharp, breathy).
- **release** — how the note ends.
- **vibrato** — oscillation of pitch or amplitude.

These are musical behaviors that performers create; MIDI can only approximate them.

---

## 🎚️ Hybrid parameters (musical concept first, technical mapping second)
These are musical ideas that can be translated into MIDI but are not defined by MIDI itself.

- **volume** — musical loudness (mapped to velocity or CC).
- **panning** — spatial placement (a mixing concept, not musical notation).
- **bend** — pitch inflection (musical gesture, mapped to pitch bend).

---

## 🎛️ Not inherently musical
These exist only because of digital systems:

- **channel** — purely a MIDI/digital routing concept.
- **onset / offset** — timestamps, not musical ideas.
